The 8-Week Program

Learn a framework, practice together, build something real

This isn't a lecture series. Each week builds on the last—stories first, then AI, then your own projects. By the end, you'll have a personal AI toolkit you actually use.

Week 0

Who Are You?

Introductions and honest conversation about where you are with AI. No judgment, no jargon—just getting on the same page as a group.

Week 1

The 4 Cs Framework (No AI Yet)

Learn the thinking framework through real stories—cooking, dating, travel. Context, Curiosity, Creative, Critical. You'll see how this loop works before touching any technology.

Week 2

Context + Curiosity (Now With AI)

Apply the first two Cs with actual AI tools. Learn how to give AI the right context and ask better questions—the two things that make everything else work.

Week 3

Creative + Critical (Build Something Real)

Use the full framework to create something meaningful. Then learn to evaluate AI output with a critical eye—what to trust, what to push back on, what to refine.

Week 4

The Tools + Practical Skills

Hands-on with the tools that actually matter. Dictation, voice input, making AI match your tone, platform personalities—the practical skills that save real time.

Week 5

Your Day-to-Day

Build your personal AI plan. Use cases for your actual work and life, "mini selves" that handle the tasks that drain you, and a clear path forward after the program.

After the Program

2 One-on-One Sessions

Two private sessions—one at 2 weeks and one at 4 weeks after the program ends. Check in on what's working, troubleshoot what's not, and keep the momentum going.

The cohort is live—8 weeks of sessions with a small group, real-time conversation, and two one-on-one sessions after. The self-paced course has the same framework and materials but you go through it on your own. If you learn better with other people, the cohort is for you.
How much time do I need each week?
Plan for about 2-3 hours: the live session itself plus some time to practice what you learned. There's no homework—just real-world application between sessions.

None. This course is built for people who aren't technical. If you can send an email and have a conversation, you have all the skills you need.
